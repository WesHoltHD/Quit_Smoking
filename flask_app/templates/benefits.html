-Immediate & Long-Term Benefits-

-After Quitting Smoking-



20 Mins After: The quickest benefit to happen within 20 mins after your last cigarette is smoked, the heart rate drops to normal. As well as the blood pressure begins to drop and circulation may improve

After 12 Hours: The body starts to cleanse itself of the excess carbon monoxide from smoking. Which will increase the bodys oxygen levels

After 1 Day: The risk of a heart-attack begins to decrease. Blood pressure drops decreasing the risk of heart disease. In a short time a person oxygen levels will rise and make physical excersize easier to do.

After 2 Days: Smoking damages the nerves associated with smell and taste. After quitting the smell and taste sensations should be hightened, from the nerves healing


Warning: Withdrawal Symptoms Typically Around Day 3

After 3 Days: Withdrawal might start to set in due to the having no nictoine in your system. Symptoms include moodiness, irritability, headaches, along with cravings.

After 1-3 Months: The lungs are starting to heal, improving lung capactiy. Former smokers may notice less coughing and a hightened ability for excersize
